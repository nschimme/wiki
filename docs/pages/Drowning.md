If you are trying to [swim](./Swim.md) on a [body of
water](./Waterways.md), and you become too
[tired](./Movement_points.md) to keep afloat, you will gulp water
and eventually **drown**.

The risk of drowning in rivers, ponds and lakes is usually quite small
for those who have [learned](./Practice.md) at least the bases of
swimming. On the contrary, the brave ones who dive into underwater
caverns, or in the depths of a lake, might find themselves
[dead](./Death.md) if they are not excellent swimmers, or if they
tarry too long.
